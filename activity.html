24-Hour Offline Hackathon

📅 20–21 February, 2026
📍 Offline (DCE Campus)
⏱️ 24 Hours
🎯 Think It. Code It. Deploy It.

Event Overview

GDG On Campus – Dronacharya College of Engineering successfully organized Code & Chaos, a flagship 24-hour offline hackathon held on 20–21 February. Built around the theme "Think It. Code It. Deploy It.", the event brought together some of the brightest student innovators to collaborate, experiment, and build impactful solutions under strict time constraints.

The hackathon served as a platform for aspiring developers to transform ideas into fully functional prototypes while competing across multiple tracks supported by industry sponsors. With participants working through the night, the event fostered creativity, teamwork, and problem-solving while simulating real-world product development environments.

Day 1: Kickoff and Round 1 Evaluation

The event commenced with registrations and an energetic inauguration, followed by the official start of the 24-hour coding marathon. Teams quickly transitioned from brainstorming to implementation, with whiteboards filled with architectures and laptops buzzing with activity.

During the first phase of evaluation, participants presented their problem statements, technical designs, and execution roadmaps. The Round 1 jury provided constructive feedback and helped teams refine their approaches.

Smash Karts Tournament

Virtual Gaming Event by GDG On Campus – DCE

Event Overview

GDG On Campus – Dronacharya College of Engineering successfully organized the Smash Karts Tournament, a virtual gaming event aimed at fostering community engagement, friendly competition, and teamwork among students. Held on 22 March, the event brought together gaming enthusiasts for an action-packed experience filled with high-speed races, strategic gameplay, and exciting challenges.

Designed to provide students with a refreshing break from academics and technical activities, the tournament encouraged participants to connect with peers, showcase their gaming skills, and enjoy an interactive competitive environment from the comfort of their homes.

The competition witnessed enthusiastic participation, with players engaging in intense battles and demonstrating exceptional gaming skills.

Gateway to GSoC 2026

Open-Source Mentorship Series by GDG On Campus – DCE

Event Overview

GDG On Campus – Dronacharya College of Engineering successfully organized Gateway to GSoC 2026, an online mentorship series aimed at empowering students with the knowledge and guidance required to begin their journey in open source and prepare for Google Summer of Code (GSoC). Conducted on 18 March 2026 via Google Meet, the event featured interactive sessions led by experienced GSoC contributors and mentors who shared valuable insights from their open-source journeys.

The initiative was designed to bridge the gap between aspiring contributors and the open-source ecosystem by providing practical guidance on project selection, proposal building, contribution workflows, and community engagement.

Learning from Experienced Contributors

The event featured two distinguished speakers from the Google Summer of Code community:

Krish Malik – GSoC 2025 Contributor

Krish Malik shared his experiences as a GSoC contributor and provided participants with practical strategies for navigating the open-source landscape. His session focused on:

  • Identifying suitable organizations and projects.
  • Understanding contribution workflows.
  • Strengthening GSoC proposals.
  • Building consistency through meaningful contributions.
  • Participating effectively in community discussions.

His insights emphasized that real learning begins when students move beyond tutorials and actively engage with communities and real-world projects.

Chirag Gupta – GSoC 2026 Mentor

Chirag Gupta offered a mentor's perspective on what distinguishes successful applications and sustainable contributions. Through examples and interactive discussions, participants gained an understanding of:

  • Criteria used by mentors during proposal evaluation.
  • Importance of project scope and feasibility.
  • Best practices for writing impactful proposals.
  • Breaking down contributions into manageable milestones.
  • Long-term involvement in open-source communities.

The session concluded with an open Q&A where students received personalized advice regarding preparation strategies and common challenges faced by first-time contributors.

Key Highlights

  • Live demonstrations of open-source contribution workflows.
  • Guidance on selecting the right organizations and projects.
  • Insights into proposal writing and mentor expectations.
  • Practical tips for making effective contributions.
  • Interactive discussions and question-and-answer sessions.
  • Exposure to the collaborative culture of open source.

Impact and Outcomes

Gateway to GSoC 2026 witnessed enthusiastic participation from students eager to explore open source and contribute to global communities. The sessions provided participants with a clearer understanding of the GSoC application process and equipped them with actionable strategies to begin their contribution journey.

By bringing together both a GSoC contributor and a mentor, the event offered attendees a comprehensive perspective—from the contributor experience to the evaluation process followed by mentors. The interaction encouraged students to view open source not merely as a program, but as a platform for continuous learning, collaboration, and community building.

Conclusion

Gateway to GSoC 2026 successfully fostered awareness and enthusiasm for open-source development among students. Through expert guidance, practical advice, and engaging discussions, the event inspired participants to take their first steps toward becoming active contributors to the global developer ecosystem.

Through initiatives like these, GDG On Campus – DCE continues to cultivate a culture of collaboration, innovation, and lifelong learning while empowering students to contribute meaningfully to the world of open source.

Devthon

Competitive Coding Challenge by GDG On Campus – DCE in Collaboration with GeeksforGeeks

Event Overview

GDG On Campus – Dronacharya College of Engineering, in collaboration with GeeksforGeeks, successfully organized Devthon, an exciting competitive coding challenge designed to test participants' problem-solving abilities, coding proficiency, and analytical thinking under pressure.

The event brought together enthusiastic programmers and aspiring developers for an intense one-hour coding battle that emphasized speed, accuracy, and logical reasoning. With a structured format and real-time competition environment, Devthon provided participants with an opportunity to showcase their technical skills and compete for a place among the top performers.

Event Format

The competition followed a carefully planned timeline to ensure a seamless experience for all participants. The portal was opened prior to the contest to facilitate smooth logins and avoid last-minute technical issues.

Participants were required to log in before the strict deadline, following which the coding challenge commenced. During the 60-minute contest, competitors tackled four carefully curated problems with a cumulative score of 100 points.

The event encouraged participants to apply algorithmic thinking, optimize solutions, and demonstrate efficiency while competing against their peers in a dynamic and challenging environment.

Key Highlights

  • Collaboration between GDG On Campus – DCE and GeeksforGeeks.
  • A one-hour coding competition designed to evaluate speed and problem-solving capabilities.
  • Four coding challenges with a total score of 100 points.
  • Real-time leaderboard and competitive environment.
  • Opportunity for participants to benchmark their performance against fellow coders.
  • A platform to strengthen programming fundamentals and enhance coding confidence.

Learning and Impact

Devthon served as more than just a competition; it provided students with valuable exposure to time-constrained problem solving and reinforced the importance of writing efficient and optimized solutions. Participants experienced the thrill of competitive programming while developing essential skills required for technical interviews, coding contests, and software development careers.

The event witnessed enthusiastic participation and fostered a spirit of healthy competition, collaboration, and continuous learning among the student community.

Conclusion

Devthon successfully brought together the coding culture of GDG On Campus – DCE and the learning ecosystem of GeeksforGeeks, creating an engaging platform for students to challenge themselves and refine their programming skills.

By encouraging analytical thinking and problem-solving under pressure, the event reinforced the commitment of GDG On Campus – DCE to nurturing technical excellence and empowering students to become confident and capable developers.
